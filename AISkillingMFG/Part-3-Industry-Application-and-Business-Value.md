# DAY 1 — "AI Across the Manufacturing Value Chain" (Where the Money Is)

### ☕ Warm-up (15 min)
Picture your product's journey: design → source → produce → inspect → ship → service. **AI can add value at every single step.** Today we walk the whole chain and pin a use case (and a dollar sign) to each link — so you can see the full map before choosing where to dig.

### 📘 Core Concepts

#### 1. The AI manufacturing value-chain map
Use this as your master menu. Each stage maps to the **See / Predict / Understand / Optimize** buckets from Part 1.

| Value-chain stage | AI use cases | Bucket | Typical value |
|-------------------|-------------|--------|---------------|
| **Design & Engineering** | Generative design, spec drafting, DOE support, knowledge search | Understand / Optimize | Faster development, captured expertise |
| **Sourcing & Procurement** | Demand forecasting, supplier risk, RFQ drafting, quote comparison | Predict / Understand | Lower inventory cost, better terms |
| **Production / Process** | Process optimization, parameter tuning, yield prediction, scheduling | Optimize / Predict | Higher throughput, less waste |
| **Quality** | Visual inspection, defect classification, auto root-cause (8D/5-Why) | See / Understand | Fewer escapes, less scrap & rework |
| **Maintenance** | Predictive maintenance, fault diagnosis from manuals, spare-parts optimization | Predict / Understand | Less downtime, longer asset life |
| **Logistics & Supply Chain** | Route/load optimization, ETA prediction, warehouse vision | Optimize / Predict | Lower freight cost, fewer stockouts |
| **Safety & Compliance** | PPE detection, hazard monitoring, auto-generated reports | See / Understand | Fewer incidents, easier audits |
| **Sustainability / Energy** | Energy optimization, emissions tracking, scrap reduction | Optimize | Lower bills, ESG goals |
| **Customer & After-sales** | Warranty analysis, service chatbots, manual Q&A | Understand | Faster service, loyalty |

> **Use this map in meetings.** When someone says "where could we use AI?", you now have a structured answer for every department.

#### 2. The "Big 5" highest-ROI starting points (proven across industry)
If you only chase five things first, chase these:
1. **Predictive Maintenance** — cut unplanned downtime (often the single biggest plant cost).
2. **AI Visual Quality Inspection** — cut scrap, rework, and escapes.
3. **Generative AI for documentation & knowledge** — reclaim 20–40% of engineers' admin time (Part 2).
4. **Demand Forecasting & Inventory Optimization** — free up working capital.
5. **Energy/Process Optimization** — direct, measurable cost savings.

#### 3. Quantifying value — the numbers that move executives
You don't need exact figures to start; you need **credible ranges** and a clear logic. Common, defensible value levers:
- **Unplanned downtime** can cost **tens of thousands of dollars per hour** on critical lines; predictive maintenance commonly reduces downtime meaningfully.
- **Scrap & rework** often run several percent of revenue; vision inspection can cut defect escapes substantially.
- **Documentation time:** knowledge workers spend a large share of the week on admin; Gen AI can halve parts of it.
- **Inventory carrying cost** typically runs ~20–30% of inventory value per year; better forecasting trims excess stock.

> **How to use these:** Take YOUR real baseline (your downtime hours, your scrap %, your inventory $) and apply a *conservative* improvement %. A conservative, well-sourced estimate beats an optimistic guess every time.

#### 4. Build vs. Buy vs. Use
Three ways to get AI capability — pick per use case:
- **Use (Crawl):** Off-the-shelf tools as-is (Copilot, a vision SaaS). Fastest, cheapest, least custom.
- **Buy (Walk):** A vendor platform configured to your plant (most predictive-maintenance and vision deployments). Best balance for most.
- **Build (Run):** Custom AI with internal/contracted data scientists. Only when it's a true competitive differentiator and you have data + talent.

> **Default advice:** **Use** to learn, **Buy** to scale, **Build** only for your "secret sauce."

### 🏭 Manufacturing Spotlight: Three Value Stories (End-to-End)
1. **Predictive maintenance on bottleneck equipment** → fewer surprise stops, maintenance scheduled into planned windows, longer asset life. *Value lever: downtime hours avoided × cost/hour.*
2. **Vision inspection on the highest-scrap line** → defects caught in real time, scrap trends visible for root cause. *Value lever: scrap % reduction × annual material cost.*
3. **Gen-AI knowledge assistant for maintenance/quality** → faster troubleshooting, retiring experts' knowledge captured. *Value lever: labor hours saved + downtime reduced.*

### 🛠️ Hands-On Workshop: "Value-Chain Heat Map" (75 min)
**Group activity, 3–5 people. Whiteboard or spreadsheet.**

1. **Draw your value chain** across the top (your real stages).
2. **Brainstorm AI use cases** under each stage (use the master map above as prompts).
3. **Score each idea** 1–5 on two axes:

4. **Plot on a 2×2 grid:** Value (Y) vs. Feasibility (X).
5. **The top-right quadrant** (high value + high feasibility) = **your shortlist.** Circle the top 3.

**Output:** A prioritized shortlist of 3 candidate projects — you'll business-case them on Day 2.

# DAY 2 — "Building the Business Case" (ROI, Risk, and Change)

### ☕ Warm-up (15 min)
A great idea with no business case dies in committee. A modest idea with a crisp, credible ROI gets funded. **Today you learn to turn your #1 candidate into a one-page case leadership will say "yes" to** — and to handle the risk and people questions they'll ask.

### 📘 Core Concepts

#### 1. The one-page AI business case (the template that gets funded)
Every funded AI project answers these eight questions on a single page:

| # | Question | Example answer |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 1 | **Problem** | "Line 3 unplanned downtime cost ~$420k last year." |
| 2 | **Proposed AI solution** | "Predictive-maintenance sensors + vendor analytics on the 4 critical motors." |
| 3 | **How it works (plain)** | "Sensors watch vibration/temp; AI predicts failures 1–2 weeks out." |
| 4 | **Expected benefit** | "Reduce unplanned downtime 25% → ~$105k/yr saved." |
| 5 | **Cost** | "$60k setup + $15k/yr. Pilot first: $20k." |
| 6 | **ROI / payback** | "Payback < 8 months; 3-yr ROI > 200%." |
| 7 | **Risks & mitigations** | "Data quality → start with 1 line; vendor support included." |
| 8 | **Ask** | "Approve a 90-day pilot on Line 3, $20k." |

> **Pro move:** Always ask for a **small pilot**, not a big rollout. Easy to approve, proves value, de-risks the big spend.

#### 2. ROI in plain math (no finance degree needed)
- **ROI = (Annual Benefit − Annual Cost) ÷ Total Investment.**
- **Payback period = Total Investment ÷ Annual Net Benefit.** (How many months to break even.)
- **Always quantify both hard and soft benefits:**
  - **Hard (easy to defend):** downtime $ avoided, scrap $ reduced, labor hours saved × loaded rate, energy $ saved.
  - **Soft (mention, don't overclaim):** faster decisions, better morale, knowledge retention, customer satisfaction.

> **Credibility tip:** Use **conservative** benefit estimates and **realistic** costs (include integration, training, and ongoing fees). An honest 150% ROI beats a fantasy 600% that nobody believes.

#### 3. The hidden costs people forget (so your case survives scrutiny)
- **Data work** (collecting, cleaning, labeling) — often the biggest hidden cost.
- **Integration** with MES/ERP/existing systems.
- **Training & change management** for the people who'll use it.
- **Ongoing** subscription, monitoring, and model upkeep (AI "drifts" — Part 1).
- **Security & compliance** review.

#### 4. Risk management — the grown-up checklist
Leadership will ask "what could go wrong?" Have answers ready:

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Bad/insufficient data** | Start where data is good; budget for data prep; pilot small |
| **AI errors / hallucinations** | Human-in-the-loop; verification steps; clear accountability |
| **Data privacy & IP leakage** | Use enterprise tools (e.g., M365 Copilot), never public tools for confidential data |
| **Over-reliance / deskilling** | Keep human judgment central; AI assists, doesn't decide alone |
| **Security** | Vendor security review; access controls; audit logs |
| **Regulatory/ethical** | Follow industry & data regulations; document decisions |
| **Model drift** | Monitor performance over time; retrain when process changes |

#### 5. AI ethics & responsible use (the trust foundation)
- **Transparency:** people should know when AI is used and how.
- **Fairness:** watch for bias (especially anything touching people — hiring, scheduling).
- **Accountability:** a human owns every AI-assisted decision.
- **Safety first:** never let AI make unsupervised safety-critical calls.
- **Workforce respect:** position AI as removing drudgery and augmenting skills — **not** as headcount reduction. *How you frame it determines whether people adopt or resist it.*

#### 6. Change management — the make-or-break factor
**Most AI projects fail on people, not technology.** Win adoption with:
- **Involve operators/engineers early** — they spot problems and become champions.
- **Start with a painful, popular problem** — solve something people *hate* doing.
- **Train hands-on** — like this course: show, don't tell.
- **Celebrate quick wins** publicly.
- **Be honest about roles** — explain how jobs evolve, address fears directly.

### 🏭 Manufacturing Spotlight: A Pilot Done Right
**Scenario:** Predictive maintenance pilot on 4 critical motors.
- **Scope:** One line, 90 days, clear metric (downtime hours).
- **Team:** 1 maintenance lead (champion), 1 engineer, vendor support.
- **Guardrail:** AI *recommends* maintenance windows; humans schedule and approve.
- **Success criteria defined up front:** ≥20% downtime reduction → approve plant-wide rollout.
- **Why it works:** small, measurable, human-led, with a pre-agreed "scale-up" trigger.

### 🛠️ Hands-On Workshop: "Pitch-Ready Business Case" (90 min)
**Individually or in pairs, build the one-page case for YOUR #1 candidate (from Day 1 homework).**

1. **Fill the 8-question template** using your real baseline numbers.
2. **Do the ROI math** — calculate payback and 3-year ROI (use Excel; have Copilot sanity-check your formula in plain language).
3. **List your top 3 risks + mitigations.**
4. **Write the "Ask"** — a small, specific pilot request.
5. **2-minute pitch:** Present to the group. Audience plays "skeptical leadership" and asks: *"How sure are you about that number?"* and *"What if it doesn't work?"* Practice answering.

**Output:** A polished, defensible one-page business case + a rehearsed pitch.

> 💡 **Tip:** Ask Copilot/ChatGPT to "review this business case as a skeptical CFO and list 5 tough questions." Then prepare answers. (Don't paste confidential numbers into public tools — use placeholders or your enterprise Copilot.)

### ✅ Key Takeaways (Day 2)
1. Use the **8-question one-page business case** — and always ask for a **small pilot.**

5. **AI projects succeed or fail on people** — invest in change management early.

# DAY 3 — "Your AI Roadmap & Capstone" (From Learner to Leader)

### ☕ Warm-up (15 min)
Three parts, nine days. You started not knowing what AI *was*. Today you'll leave with a **written roadmap** and a **capstone project** you can put in front of your boss this week. **This is where learning becomes leadership.**

### 📘 Core Concepts

#### 1. The 90-day AI starter roadmap (a template you can reuse)

| Phase | Timeframe | Focus | Key actions |
|-------|-----------|-------|-------------|
| **Phase 0 — Foundation** | Weeks 1–2 | Get ready | Pick the pilot, name a champion, secure data access, set the metric |
| **Phase 1 — Pilot** | Weeks 3–10 | Prove value small | Run on ONE line/area, human-in-the-loop, track the metric weekly |
| **Phase 2 — Evaluate** | Weeks 11–12 | Decide | Compare to success criteria; document results, costs, lessons |
| **Phase 3 — Scale or Stop** | Week 13+ | Commit | If it hit the target → scale to more lines/plants; if not → learn and pivot |

> **The "Scale or Stop" gate is your friend.** Pre-agreeing the success bar removes politics from the decision.

#### 2. Building an AI-ready organization (beyond one project)
- **People:** Grow AI literacy (this course!), identify champions, plan role evolution.
- **Data:** Treat data as an asset — improve collection, labeling, and access. *No data, no AI.*
- **Governance:** Clear policies on approved tools, data privacy, human-in-the-loop, and accountability.
- **Infrastructure:** Connectivity (sensors/IoT), integration with MES/ERP, secure cloud or edge compute.
- **Culture:** Reward experimentation, tolerate smart failures, celebrate wins.

#### 3. The AI maturity ladder (where is your plant — and where next?)

| Level | Description | Your next step |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **1. Aware** | Talking about AI, no projects | Run a pilot (you're ready!) |
| **2. Active** | 1–2 pilots running | Standardize what works; build governance |
| **3. Operational** | AI in daily use on several lines | Integrate with core systems; scale teams |
| **4. Systemic** | AI embedded across the value chain | Optimize end-to-end; AI in strategy |
| **5. Transformative** | AI is a core competitive advantage | Continuous innovation; new business models |

> Most manufacturers are at **Level 1–2.** Your goal after this course: **move your area from Aware to Active** with one solid pilot.

#### 4. Governance & responsible scaling (do this before you grow)
- **Approved-tools list** (which AI tools are sanctioned, especially for company data).
- **Data classification** (what can/can't go into which tools).
- **Human-in-the-loop policy** for consequential decisions.
- **Monitoring** for accuracy and drift; a retraining plan.
- **An accountable owner** for each AI system.
- **Audit trail** for agent actions and AI-assisted decisions.

#### 5. Staying current without the hype (a calm operating stance)
- **Follow your core vendors** (Microsoft, your MES/ERP/maintenance platforms) — they ship AI features you already own.
- **Re-evaluate quarterly** — the tools improve fast; what wasn't possible last quarter may be cheap now.
- **Separate signal from noise** — ignore "AGI is here" headlines; focus on "what reliably saves us money/time/risk today."
- **Keep humans in charge** — your durable advantage is judgment + AI, not AI alone.

#### 6. The future, briefly (so you're not blindsided)
- **More capable agents** doing multi-step workflows (Part 2) — supervised, integrated into business systems.
- **AI + IoT + robotics** converging on the "smart factory."
- **Easier no-code tools** putting AI-building in the hands of engineers, not just data scientists.
- **AGI** remains a long-horizon, debated topic — **plan for capable narrow AI, watch AGI from the sidelines.**

> **This capstone is real work, not a drill.** Polish it and you have something to actually propose at your plant.
